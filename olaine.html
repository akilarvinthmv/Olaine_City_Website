City Overview

Olaine is a city in Olaine County, the administrative center of Olaine ovada. It is 25 km from Latvia the capital Riga (center), 20 km from the Latvian state town of Jelgava, as well as 4.5 km from the settlement of the other largest district of Olaines – Virolaines. Olaine is one of the two administrative areas of the Olaine region – The city of Olaine borders on all sides Olaine Parish. The city of Olaine covers an area of 6.84 km².

Two rivers – Olainite and Pupla flow through the city. The city of Olaine has its own symbolism: coat of arms, flag and song. The coat of arms of the city of Olaine was confirmed on November 12, 1998. The coat of arms shield is diagonally divided into two parts, silver and green from the top of the left. The silver part shows three red blood blisters, two in the upper row and one in the bottom. Blood blisters symbolize health and life because the city houses a pharmaceutical factory “Olainfarm” (founded in 1972). The green part shows the silver leaves of a black cranberry coat and red berries that symbolize the peat swamps in Olaine's county. The artists of the coat of arms are Juris Ivanov, Ilze Liebiete, Imant Purhe. The flag of the city of Olaine is in a rectangular shape with two vertical bands of equal width: the left side is red, the right side – green, but in the middle – the image of the city's coat of arms. The flag was approved in 21, 2016. in December. “The authors of the text of the Olaine song” are Eve Sircova and Atis Auzan, while the composer – Atis Auzan.

History

The city of Olaine was established in Olaine Parish, a swampy neighborhood that later played a key role in the development of the city. Olaine Parish was established in 1819 and belonged to the 1635 congregation of Katlakalna and Olaine. In 1638, the first Lutheran church was built in the Scad, named after St. Olaf, from whom the name of Olaine later developed. Several manors (including Olaine Manor) and farms were built in the modern village of Olaine, Jaunolain and Ezīša near the road Riga–Jelgava. The city of Olaine was located in a swampy area, with only a few households and a cemetery. The area began to develop when the railway line Riga (from Tower Hill)–Jelgava was built in 1868 and a railway station was built opposite the center of Olaine Manor. 1871.a year in modern Newolaine, the house of the parish of Olaine was built and the center of the parish was formed. World War I During the time, Olaine Parish, including the Olaine neighborhood, was destroyed. After the war, the station, the parish house, the Lutheran church and other buildings were restored, as well as the name Olaine was given to denoting the area. In 1939, peat mining began in the Bluetah swamp in Olaine Parish (work performed a/s “Kudra”). In 1940, the first apartment house was built, which also housed the administration of a/s “Kudra”.

During World War II, Olaine's surroundings were destroyed again. After the war, a peat factory and a working village were built in the area and a prisoner-of-war camp was set up until 1952. On December 31, 1945, a council of the village of Olaine was established in the parish of Olaine, and on December 31, 1949, the district of Riga was established, which includes the council of the village of Olaine. On July 25, 1951, the Council of the village of Baloses was abolished, adding its territory to the territory of the Olaine village council. 20th century. In the 1950s, the construction of public buildings and apartment buildings began in Olaine. In 1950, the National Institute for Urban Construction developed a plan for the workers' village of the Olaine peat plant, with a building capacity of 2,000 inhabitants. In 1958, in Moscow, the Council of Ministers of the Union of the Soviet Socialist Republics (PSRS) decided to build chemical plants near the peat factory “Olaine”. The first chemical plant in Olaine was an adhesive factory, which was established in 1959. In 1961, the construction of a peat briquette factory was completed. 20th century. In the early 1960s, construction volumes grew, infrastructure developed, a kindergarten and a school was built, and in the late 1960s – cinema, a shopping center and a catering complex.

On June 12, 1963, the territory of the Soviet farm of the village council of Dalbe in the district of Riga “Olaine” was added to the territory of the village council of Olaine, and the collective farm of the village council of Olaine “Daugava” and part of the country's forest fund was added to the village council area of Katlakalna. On May 24, 1965, Olaine was granted the status of the townspeople, and on February 20, 1967, – city law. In 1965, the territory of the parish of Olaine was added to the parish of Dalbe and the parish of Olaine was liquidated. Rapid construction continued in 20th century. In the 1970s and 1980s, when dozens of apartment houses, kindergartens, Olaine's 2nd high school, a sports house and grocery stores were completed. Due to limited funding, it became more difficult to develop and maintain urban infrastructure, as was water supply for factories. In 1972, an Olaine chemical pharmaceutical plant was established.

After Latvia's restoration of independence In 1990, the parish of Olaine was restored. In 1992, the issue of environmental pollution caused by factories was raised. The Supreme Council of the Republic of Latvia forbade the development of an industrial zone near the city of Olaine until environmental pollution problems were addressed. The construction of residential houses, on the other hand, was slowed by the lack of workers and the economic crisis. In 2004, a new plan for the city area was developed, while Latvia joined In the European Union provided opportunities for funding for urban development. In 2009, the Olaine region was established with a center in Olaine.

Destination

Olaine is home to a World War I history cognitive route, as well as an interactive cultural route with elements of the supplemented reality “Blockhouse stories”. In 2010, the Olaine Forest Park, which houses playgrounds, a rope track “Forest cat”, a Basque trail, outdoor trainers and bike lanes, stage was completed. The intended object of tourism is a water tower decorated with a graphic drawing, as well as other interesting objects.

The development of Olaine's economy was historically influenced by peat mining and the development of peat briquettes, as well as the development of industry (especially chemical). In and around Olaine, there are chemical companies, such as Olaine is considered the center of production of Latvian chemical products. The main sectors after turnover in Olaine and Olaine County include manufacturing, industrial production, pharmaceutical production, metalworking, chemical industry, equipment and building materials production, waste recycling. The freight transport and logistics, warehousing services are also a significant industry.

The largest companies in Olaine is a/s “Olainfarm” and a/s “Olaine Chemical Plant “BIOLARS”” (production of chemicals, registered on 31 January 1995). Olaine is home to a logistics company “Nordic Industrial Park” (registered in 1998).

Catering services in Olaine are provided by restaurants, pizzeria, as well as fast food restaurants. Accommodation is provided by a service hotel, as well as several guest houses in Olaine Parish. Various service providers are available in the city, such as a car service, a beauty salon.

Cultural-institutions

The Olaine Cultural Center (established in 1996) is actively organizing events throughout the county and has a variety of self-operating teams: mixed choir “Song” (created in 1966), female vocal ensembles “Undine” (created in 2000) and “(TAG1> middle generation dance team “Janita” (established in 1995), exotic dance team “Oriental Flowers” (established in 2010), children's dance team “Oli” (formed in 2004). In 2019, the Olaine Museum of History and Art (founded on November 22, 1995) was reconstructed and features a number of exhibitions, such as the history of the county and the extraction of peat. The Olaine City Library (1965) operates in Olaine.

Sports

The Olaine Sports Center has three stadiums (Olaines Stadium, Stadium at Olaine High School 2, New Launches Stadium), two sports houses (Olaines Sports House, Jaulaine Sports House), the Olaine Swimming Pool, two skate parks (Olaines and Newolaines), as well as the Olaine Slate. In winter, Mount Winter is available in Olaine, in summer – active recreation park in Owake. There is a mototrase in Olaine, as well as an asphalt velotrase. Notable Olaines athletes: Gunta Bashko (basketball player), John Kuzmin (slalom skateboarder), Arthur Liskov (slalom skateboarder), Dmitry Milkevich (lightened).
